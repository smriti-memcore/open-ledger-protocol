# OLP IFRS/IAS Standard Mapping (Dubai CPA Guide)

While the OLP specification references US GAAP (ASC standards), the UAE (and Dubai specifically) mandates **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)**. 

Because US GAAP and IFRS are highly converged on transaction-level accounting, a Dubai-based CPA will easily understand OLP when mapped to IFRS/IAS equivalents:

| OLP Feature / US GAAP Standard | US GAAP Code | IFRS / IAS Equivalent | Core Concept Compliance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Revenue Recognition** | ASC 606 | **IFRS 15** | 5-step revenue recognition model; distinct performance obligations (POBs), transaction price allocation, point-in-time vs. over-time ratable recognition. |
| **Sales Returns Reserves** | ASC 606-10-55-22 | **IFRS 15 (Appendix B)** | Variable consideration; recognizing a refund liability and asset for right to recover returned goods instead of booking 100% immediate sales revenue. |
| **Capitalized Contract Costs** | ASC 340-40 | **IFRS 15 (91-104)** | Capitalization of incremental costs of obtaining a contract (sales commissions) and systematic amortization matching revenue transfer. |
| **Foreign Exchange Matters** | ASC 830 | **IAS 21** | Defining functional currency, translating foreign transaction amounts, and booking realized exchange gains/losses upon settlement. |
| **Consolidated Accounts** | ASC 810 | **IFRS 10** | Accounting for parent-subsidiary relationships and compiling elimination entries to clear intercompany balances. |
| **Segment Reporting** | ASC 280 | **IFRS 8** | Divisional reporting by brand/line of business segments for external presentation. |
